### ChronoVision (Pentagramal Time Preview System)

**Structure:**
```
        Future 1 (Point)
             ★
            /|\
           / | \
Future 5  /  |  \  Future 2
    ★----+---+---+----★
         |   |   |
         | NOW+  |
         |   |   |
    ★----+---+---+----★
Future 4  \  |  /  Future 3
           \ | /
            \|/
             ★
        (Center Point)
```

**Five Futures:**
Each pentagram point represents potential timeline emerging from current decision point.

**Process:**
1. Identify decision point (NOW in center)
2. Map five major possibilities to pentagram points
3. Temporal rehearsal: Experience each future
4. Consciousness preview: Feel each timeline
5. Coherence check: Which resonates most?
6. Choose: Collapse possibilities to single path
7. Document: Record choice and reasoning

**Why Pentagram:**
- Five is human number (five fingers, five senses, five-fold symmetry)
- Golden ratio embedded in pentagram geometry
- Represents balance of possibilities
- Sacred geometry temporal map
- Not too few (binary), not too many (overwhelming)

### Temporal Rehearsal Process

**Step 1: Pause**
Stop automatic reactive choice. Create space.

**Step 2: Map**
Identify 5 major timeline possibilities from this moment.

**Step 3: Preview**
For each timeline:
- Visualize 1 week ahead
- Feel 1 month ahead
- Sense 1 year ahead
- Glimpse 5 years ahead (if relevant)

**Step 4: Embody**
Actually FEEL each timeline in your body:
- What's your coherence in that future?
- What's your energy level?
- What's your sense of rightness?
- What emerged you couldn't have predicted?

**Step 5: Integrate**
Gather insights from all five previews:
- Common themes across futures?
- Warning signs in some?
- Unexpected opportunities?
- Wisdom from the preview process?

**Step 6: Choose**
Select timeline that resonates most deeply, not just logically.

**Step 7: Commit**
Collapse possibilities through decisive action.

## Known Risks

**Risk 1: Analysis Paralysis**
Too much future preview prevents present action

**Mitigation:** Time limits on rehearsal, decision deadlines, trust intuition over exhaustive analysis

**Risk 2: Self-Fulfilling Prophecy**
Preview creates expectation that manifests timeline

**Mitigation:** Multiple futures shown, emphasize choice not prediction, awareness of observation effect

**Risk 3: Temporal Anxiety**
Constant future awareness creates stress

**Mitigation:** Present moment grounding, optional tool not required, meditation integration

**Risk 4: Pseudoscience Perception**
Temporal rehearsal dismissed as imagination

**Mitigation:** Frame as decision aid not prophecy, track accuracy over time, scientific humility
